  • Best if you like the current mobile-first flow.

Option 2: Real Oval Table

A more immersive table: seats sit around a true felt oval, with the pot and board at the center and your controls anchored below.

  • Feels the most like a poker table.
  • Requires careful seat positioning across phone sizes.
  • Good direction if visual atmosphere matters most.

Option 3: Modern Game HUD

A gameplay-first interface with a clear status rail, visible recent action, hand-strength meter, and a centered board. This favors clarity over casino realism.

  • Best for repeated play and fast decisions.
  • Makes AI actions and pot pressure easier to read.
  • My recommended direction for a smarter-feeling app.

Option 5: One-Hand Mobile

An aggressive mobile-first layout with giant hole cards, thumb-zone controls, and a bottom betting sheet. It treats phone ergonomics as the main product constraint.

  • Best for one-handed play.
  • Most focused on the player's own decision.
  • Opponent detail is intentionally compressed.
